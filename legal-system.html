Legal System Background

Our legal system is designed to maintain order and ensure justice for all citizens. Explore the key laws, penalties, and the structure of our judicial system.

  • The Court System
    • Supreme Court: The highest authority that oversees major cases, including those involving severe penalties like the death penalty.
    • Lower Courts: Handle less severe cases, such as minor infractions or first-time offenses.
  • Roles
    • Judges: Appointed to oversee the trials. The Supreme Court Judge handles major cases, while Lower Court Judges handle minor cases.
    • Jury (Optional): A group of impartial players selected to decide the verdict based on the evidence presented. This could be implemented for more complex cases.
    • Executioners (if applicable): Players tasked with carrying out the death penalty.
  • Law Enforcement
    • Police Force: Responsible for investigating crimes, arresting suspects, and bringing them to trial. Manage the jail and ensure that prisoners serve their sentences.
  • Trial Procedures
    • Investigation: Once a rule is broken, the Police Force investigates the incident. If sufficient evidence is found, the suspect is arrested and brought to trial.
    • Trial:
      • Hearing: Both sides present their arguments.
      • Deliberation: If there is a jury, they deliberate and reach a verdict.
      • Verdict: The Judge or Jury announces the verdict (Guilty or Not Guilty).
      • Sentencing: If found guilty, the Judge assigns the penalty based on the rule violated.
  • Penalties
    • No Gambling: Penalty: All money confiscated + 1 day jail.
    • No Killing Other Players: Penalty: Death penalty + 2 days jail.
    • Disrespecting Other Country Players in Chat: Penalty: 2 days jail.
    • Having More Than 10k In-Game: Penalty: 2 hours jail.
    • No Griefing: Penalty: Severe, including potential bans.
    • Tax Evasion: Penalty: 2days jail, All money taken, Comunity Service .
    • (Note: The penalties can be adjusted based on the severity and context of the crime.)
  • Appeals
    • Appeal Process: If a player believes they were unjustly convicted, they may appeal to the Supreme Court. The appeal will be reviewed, and if sufficient evidence is provided, a retrial may be granted.
