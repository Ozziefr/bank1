The Founding Era and 12th Century

Late 100s - Early 200s: The Formation of Fredonia

Late 100s: The settlement of Fredonia was established by five barbarians led by King Xyz. This group of settlers chose a strategic location near fertile lands and a defensible hill, ideal for building a new society. King Xyz implemented rudimentary laws and organized a small, but functional, community.

Early 200s: Fredonia began to attract new settlers from surrounding areas. As the population grew, the settlement evolved into a burgeoning kingdom. King Xyz continued to strengthen the nascent nation through diplomatic alliances with neighboring tribes and by consolidating internal power.

Early 300s - Mid 500s: Expansion and Stabilization

305: The kingdom experienced its first major external conflict with the Siege of the Northern Fort, where Fredonia successfully defended its territory against a coalition of neighboring tribes. This victory secured Fredonia’s position and led to a period of relative peace.

340: King Alden, a successor of King Xyz, initiated the Great Expansion Campaign. This campaign aimed to extend Fredonia’s borders and integrate nearby lands into the kingdom. The campaign was largely successful, resulting in increased agricultural production and economic prosperity.

470: The Cultural Renaissance of Fredonia began, marked by significant advancements in art, architecture, and governance. During this period, the kingdom built impressive structures, such as fortified walls and grand halls, and developed a sophisticated legal system.

Early 600s - Early 800s: Prosperity and Internal Challenges

614: The Conflict of the Eastern Tribes emerged when eastern tribes rebelled against Fredonia’s expanding influence. The conflict was resolved through a combination of military campaigns and diplomatic negotiations, resulting in the tribes accepting Fredonian rule in exchange for autonomy.

675: King Valerius implemented the Land Reformation Act, which aimed to redistribute land more equitably among the population. This act was designed to address internal discontent and enhance agricultural efficiency.

725: The Revolt of the Merchant Class occurred as a result of economic inequalities and high taxation. The revolt was led by a coalition of influential merchants who sought greater representation and economic reforms. The revolt was eventually quelled, but it led to reforms that included the establishment of a council of advisors to represent various social classes.

Early 900s - Early 12th Century: Consolidation and Innovation

912: The War of the Three Rivers began as Fredonia sought to expand its control over the strategically important river valleys. The war saw a series of battles with rival kingdoms and ended with Fredonia securing control over the river regions, boosting its trade and economic influence.

950: The Great Library of Fredonia was established, becoming a center for learning and innovation. The library attracted scholars and intellectuals from across the continent, fostering advancements in science, literature, and technology.

1020: The Reformation of the Legal Code was carried out under King Leopold. This reform modernized the kingdom’s legal system, introducing new laws and regulations to better manage the growing population and complexities of governance.

1085: The Invasion of the Northern Clans posed a significant threat to Fredonia. The northern clans, seeking to capitalize on internal instability, launched a series of raids. Fredonia’s military, under the command of General Marcus, successfully repelled the invaders and reinforced its northern defenses.

1120: The Economic Boom of Fredonia occurred as the kingdom’s trade routes expanded and its economy flourished. Innovations in agriculture and trade led to increased prosperity and a higher standard of living for many Fredonians.

1175: King Alaric initiated the Reformation of the Realm, a comprehensive overhaul of the kingdom’s legal and economic systems. This reform aimed to consolidate power, address internal issues, and prepare Fredonia for future challenges.

12th Century - 1499: The Era of Blood and War

1200-1250: The kingdom entered a period marked by relentless conflicts known as the Wars of the Crimson Tide. These wars were characterized by brutal battlesand constant invasions by hostile neighboring states. Fredonia faced multiple fronts of attack, which strained its resources and military.

1265: The Siege of the Capital was one of the darkest periods in Fredonia’s history. Enemy forces, taking advantage of the kingdom's weakened state, advanced towards the capital. The city was besieged and suffered severe damage. The Fredonian defenders, under the command of the legendary hero Leonard Opalti, were pushed to the brink of defeat.

1270: Leonard Opalti emerged as a beacon of hope. In the Battle of the Last Stand, Leonard, with a small regiment of men 4000~ man, faced overwhelming enemy forces(27600+ man from 3 different countries and multiple tribes gathered). Despite the odds, he led a fierce counterattack, personally killing over 2,700 enemies and turning the tide of the battle. His leadership and bravery inspired the remaining troops to retake the stolen lands and push back the invaders.

1275: The Treaty of Ashen Fields was signed, marking the end of the wars. The treaty forced Fredonia to concede 30% of its territory to the victorious enemy. The kingdom, though battered, began a slow process of recovery and rebuilding. The peace treaty marked the end of the bloodshed but left Fredonia with significant territorial losses and internal challenges.The crowning of the Hero helped to give the population hope for the future hard times.

1300-1400: Fredonia’s recovery from the wars was gradual. The kingdom focused on rebuilding its infrastructure and economy, restoring stability, and re-establishing its borders. During this period, Fredonia saw improvements in agriculture and trade, which helped stabilize its economy.

1440: The assasination of King Leonard III, the grandson of the legendary hero, triggered a succession crisis. The resulting civil strife, known as the Civil War of the Filthy Lords, further destabilized the kingdom and broke it to create 2 new countries The Free Tribes Union in the north and Kingdom of Finland in the south.

1490: The kingdom of Finland faced the Invasion of the Free Tribes Union . The Free Tribes, launched new raids. The Finish military, though weakened from previous conflicts, managed to repel the invaders and managed with the help of the second national hero Finn Hakon That reunited Fredonia Under a new banner New Kingdom of Finland, which eventually led to losses on both sides.

1499: The Final Reforms of the Realm were initiated by the ruling monarch Finn Hakon That. These reforms aimed to address the kingdom’s ongoing issues. These reforms aimed at stabilizing the political situation and preparing for future challenges, setting the stage for the eventual transition to a new era under King Ozzie.
